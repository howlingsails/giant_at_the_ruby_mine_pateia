As a druid with a quarterstaff and a light hammer, you have several options for attacking the owlbear in D&D. Here are a few attack scenarios:
Scenario 1: You decide to use your quarterstaff against the owlbear. You roll a d20 to hit, adding your attack bonus. Roll to Hit: d20 + Attack Bonus If you successfully hit, you roll damage with your quarterstaff. Roll for Damage: 1d8 + Strength Modifier The owlbear's reaction will depend on the amount of damage dealt. It may retaliate or take defensive actions, such as attacking you or attempting to dodge your next attack.
Scenario 2: In another instance, you opt to use your light hammer against the owlbear. Again, you roll a d20 to hit, adding your attack bonus. Roll to Hit: d20 + Attack Bonus If your attack hits, you roll damage with your light hammer. Roll for Damage: 1d4 + Strength Modifier The owlbear's reaction will vary based on the amount of damage inflicted. It might become more aggressive or attempt to retreat. Remember, the specific attack bonus and strength modifier values will depend on your character's abilities and proficiency. Additionally, the owlbear's armor class and hit points will affect your chances of hitting and the amount of damage you can deal.
Tactics
In this scenario, let's assume the druid and their bard parents are in a pool without any armor or weapons, with rocks and their spellcasting abilities available. Here are four tactics they might employ against the owlbear:
Tactic 1: Distraction and Rock Throw The bard parents decide to use their spells to create distracting illusions, while the druid picks up rocks from the poolside and throws them at the owlbear.
Bard Action:
Parent 1 casts Minor Illusion to create the sound of a loud predator nearby, distracting the owlbear.
Parent 2 casts Prestidigitation to create flashing lights in the owlbear's peripheral vision, further disorienting it.
Druid Action:
The druid picks up rocks and throws them at the owlbear to keep it off balance.
Roll to Hit with Rock:
d20 + Dexterity Modifier
If the rock hits, roll for improvised weapon damage.
Roll for Damage with Rock:
1d4 + Strength Modifier
The DM might roll for the owlbear's reaction, possibly taking damage or becoming momentarily distracted by the illusions and rock throws.
Tactic 2: Enchantment and Push
The bard parents use enchantment spells to try and affect the owlbear's mind, while the druid attempts to push it back into the pool.
Bard Action:
Parent 1 casts Charm Person on the owlbear, attempting to make it friendly or less hostile.
Parent 2 casts Friends to try and influence the owlbear's attitude towards them.
Druid Action:
The druid moves behind the owlbear and attempts to push it back into the pool.
Roll to Push the Owlbear:
Strength (Athletics) Check (opposed by the owlbear's Strength or Dexterity check)
The DM determines the owlbear's reaction to the enchantment spells and the druid's attempt to push it.
Tactic 3: Spellcasting and Evasion (Updated) The bard parents use their spellcasting abilities to hinder the owlbear's movements, while the druid focuses on evasion and keeping a safe distance. Bard Action:
Parent 1 casts Bane on the owlbear, attempting to weaken its attacks and saving throws by imposing a penalty on them.
Parent 2 casts Vicious Mockery, insulting and distracting the owlbear to impose disadvantage on its next attack roll.
Druid Action:
The druid uses their mobility and nature-based spells to stay agile and keep a safe distance from the owlbear.
The DM rolls for the owlbear's saving throw against Bane and determines its reaction to the insults and distractions caused by Vicious Mockery. The owlbear's attack rolls may be made with disadvantage due to Vicious Mockery.
Tactic 4: Healing and Support
The bard parents focus on healing and providing support to the druid, boosting their abilities and helping them endure the owlbear's attacks.
Bard Action:
Parent 1 casts Healing Word to restore the druid's hit points.
Parent 2 uses the Help action, giving the druid advantage on their next attack roll against the owlbear.
Druid Action:
The druid takes advantage of the support received and attacks the owlbear using their natural abilities or available spells.
The DM determines the effectiveness of the healing spell and the druid's attack roll against the owlbear, considering the advantage granted by the Help action.
Remember, the specific rolls and outcomes will depend on the characters' abilities, the owlbear's statistics, and the DM's discretion in the game.
Action 5: Animal Friendship The druid attempts to use their spellcasting ability to befriend the owlbear and convince it to become non-hostile.
Druid Action:
The druid casts Animal Friendship, trying to magically convince the owlbear that they mean no harm and should be trusted.
Roll for Spell Save DC:
The owlbear must make a Wisdom saving throw against the druid's spell save DC.
The DM determines the owlbear's reaction based on the outcome of the saving throw and the druid's spellcasting ability.
Action 6: Calming Tones and Retreat The bard parents use their calming voices and soothing melodies to try to pacify the owlbear, while the druid takes the opportunity to retreat.
Bard Action:
Parent 1 uses their Bardic Inspiration ability to inspire the owlbear with calming tones and melodies, attempting to soothe its aggression.
Parent 2 casts Dissonant Whispers at a low level, aiming to create a jarring sound that may cause the owlbear to flee.
Druid Action:
The druid takes the opportunity while the bard parents are calming the owlbear to slowly and stealthily retreat from the area.
The DM determines the owlbear's response to the calming tones and dissonant whispers. The success of the retreat depends on the druid's ability to move silently and the owlbear's perception.
Action 7: Offer Food and Peaceful Gestures
The druid and bard parents attempt to establish peace by offering the owlbear food and making peaceful gestures.
Druid and Bard Actions:
The druid and bard parents gather fruits or other food items and slowly approach the owlbear, making gentle, non-threatening gestures. They offer the food to the owlbear, showing their peaceful intentions.
The DM determines the owlbear's reaction based on its level of hostility, hunger, and the characters' approach.
Action 8: Hide and Camouflage The druid uses their druidcraft ability to create natural camouflage for themselves and the bard parents, allowing them to hide and escape the owlbear's notice.
Druid Action:
The druid uses druidcraft to create natural illusions, such as falling leaves or the sound of animals, to distract and confuse the owlbear's senses. They find suitable cover or foliage to hide themselves and the bard parents, blending in with their surroundings.
The DM determines the owlbear's perception checks to detect the characters' hiding and camouflage attempts. The success of the escape relies on the owlbear's perception and the characters' ability to remain hidden.
Remember, these actions and their outcomes are subject to the rules of the game, the characters' abilities, the owlbear's behavior, and the DM's decisions in the specific campaign.

Cure Wounds

Spell Level: 1st level
School: Evocation
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: Touch
Components: V, S
Duration: Instantaneous

Description: As you touch a creature, its wounds begin to close, and it regains hit points. The target regains 1d8 + your spellcasting ability modifier in hit points.

Example: As the owlbear's claw slashes across your friend's chest, you rush forward and place your hand on their wound. You channel your druidic energy, and a soothing light emanates from your palm, knitting their flesh together. The wound closes, and your friend gasps in relief as they regain 7 hit points.

Entangle

Spell Level: 1st level
School: Conjuration
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: 90 feet
Components: V, S
Duration: Concentration, up to 1 minute

Description: Plants in a 20-foot square area become magically entwined, making the ground difficult terrain for creatures within the area. Additionally, creatures of your choice within the area must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be restrained by the entangling plants.

Example: Sensing the owlbear charging toward you, you raise your hands and summon the forces of nature. Vines burst forth from the ground, wrapping around the owlbear's legs and hindering its movements. It struggles against the entangling plants, trying to break free, but remains restrained for now.

Faerie Fire

Spell Level: 1st level
School: Evocation
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: 60 feet
Components: V
Duration: Concentration, up to 1 minute

Description: You create a magical light within a 20-foot cube. Any creature within the area must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or be outlined in light. The outlined creatures shed dim light, cannot benefit from invisibility, and attackers have advantage on attack rolls against them.

Example: Sensing the owlbear's stealthy movements in the dense forest, you raise your hands and summon the essence of faerie light. The owlbear, previously hidden in the shadows, is now bathed in a soft glow, its outline clearly visible. Your allies take advantage of the newfound visibility and launch a coordinated attack against the owlbear, their strikes finding their mark more easily.

Thunderwave

Spell Level: 1st level
School: Evocation
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: Self (15-foot cube)
Components: V, S
Duration: Instantaneous

Description: A wave of thunderous force sweeps out from you. Each creature in a 15-foot cube originating from you must make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 2d8 thunder damage and is pushed 10 feet away from you. On a successful save, the creature takes half the damage and isn't pushed.

Example: Feeling the owlbear closing in, you take a deep breath and release a primal roar. A shockwave of thunder erupts from your position, shaking the ground and deafening those nearby. The owlbear is caught in the blast, thrown backward by the force and momentarily stunned, giving your allies a precious moment to reposition and strike.

Duration: Instantaneous

Bane

Spell Level: 1st level
School: Enchantment
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: 30 feet
Components: V, S, M (a drop of blood)
Duration: Concentration, up to 1 minute

Description: Up to three creatures of your choice within range must make Charisma saving throws. Whenever a target that fails the saving throw makes an attack roll or a saving throw before the spell ends, they must roll a d4 and subtract the number rolled from the attack roll or saving throw.

Example: As the owlbear charges towards you and your allies, you chant an incantation while flicking a drop of blood into the air. A dark aura envelops the creature, causing it to hesitate for a moment. The bane takes hold, and whenever the owlbear tries to attack or resist your spells, a nagging voice echoes in its mind, filling it with doubt and causing it to falter.

Dissonant Whispers

Spell Level: 1st level
School: Enchantment
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: 60 feet
Components: V
Duration: Instantaneous

Description: You whisper a discordant melody that only one creature of your choice within range can hear, wracking it with terrible pain. The target must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, it takes 3d6 psychic damage and must immediately use its reaction to move as far away from you as possible.

Example: As the owlbear draws closer, its eyes locked on you, you take a deep breath and let out an eerie, haunting melody. The sound pierces through the creature's mind, causing it to wince in agony and fear. It breaks away from you, frantically retreating, unable to bear the tormenting whispers that echo in its head, taking 13 points of psychic damage in the process.

Shillelagh

Spell Level: Cantrip
School: Transmutation
Casting Time: 1 bonus action
Range: Touch
Components: V, S, M (mistletoe, a shamrock leaf, and a club or quarterstaff)
Duration: 1 minute

Description: You imbue a wooden club or quarterstaff with magical energy. For the duration, you can use your spellcasting ability instead of Strength for the attack and damage rolls of melee attacks using that weapon, and the weapon's damage becomes a d8. The weapon also becomes magical if it isn't already.

Example: As you grip your quarterstaff, you mutter ancient words and trace symbols in the air with mistletoe and a shamrock leaf. A vibrant green glow envelops the weapon, imbuing it with magical power. Your quarterstaff feels lighter, and as you swing it towards the owlbear, it connects with a resounding thud, dealing 7 points of damage.

Speak with Animals

Spell Level: 1st level
School: Divination
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: Self
Components: V, S
Duration: 10 minutes

Description: For the duration, you can communicate with animals and they can understand you. You can ask questions, relay simple messages, and receive information from them. However, the spell doesn't grant animals any special intelligence or understanding beyond their natural capabilities.

Example: As you find yourself facing the imposing owlbear, you take a deep breath and connect with the essence of nature around you. Suddenly, you can perceive the thoughts and emotions of the animals nearby. You lock eyes with a nearby squirrel and ask for its assistance in distracting the owlbear, conveying your plan. The squirrel chitters in response, scampering away to execute the strategy while you prepare for the encounter.
